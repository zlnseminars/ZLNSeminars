The Crown: The Jewel of Jealousy-Part Three

It’s interesting how once God changes your life and transforms you that you begin to come across those who are exhibiting the same toxic behaviors you once had. As God began to work on me and my perception of self changed, I got my authentic fine jewel back. As I matured in my spiritual walk I encountered many trials and many blessings. I encountered people who thought I didn’t deserve the blessings God gave me. The same jewel I had once possessed in my crown was in theirs and I knew it was an opportunity to reveal the true value of my crown.

Surprisingly that counterfeit jewel was in the crown of some closest to me. At first I was angry and hurt but, I realized I couldn’t act on those feelings and risk exchanging another authentic jewel . I had to feel, acknowledge and then release those emotions and act out love and patience better yet ,understanding. I knew they were hurting inside. I knew because I had been them. I knew that they didn’t understand the hell I had been through to obtain the blessings in my life. They didn’t understand how many nights I cried and worked and lost sleep, worried. They didn’t see all that.

Heavy is the head that wears the crown.

All of our crowns come with authentic fine jewels but also with some weight. That weight is the trials we endure, the losses, the heartbreaks, the setbacks and failures.We all have to bear the weight of our crowns. Many seek to take it, but what they fail to realize is that our crowns are heavy with positions, situations, and experiences that only we can bear. If they were to ever walk in our shoes, they’d fall and collapse under the pressure . The same goes for us. We wouldn’t be able to carry the weight of their crown. Each crown is uniquely designed for the individual it is assigned. All are priceless!

However, it’s up to us to maintain its value. The thing is that we must see and understand our value and once we see it we must guard it heavily.
